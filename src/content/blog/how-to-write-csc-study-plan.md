## Why Your Study Plan Can Make or Break Your CSC Application

The Study Plan (研修计划) is one of the **most important documents** in your CSC Scholarship application. While your GPA and test scores are fixed, your Study Plan is where you have full control to impress the selection committee.

A strong study plan does three things:
1. **Shows clear academic goals** — What exactly do you want to study and why?
2. **Demonstrates why China** — Why this specific university and supervisor?
3. **Outlines a realistic timeline** — What will you accomplish semester by semester?

Most rejected applicants fail on the study plan. Here is how to write one that stands out.

---

## Study Plan Structure (800–1,500 Words)

### Section 1: Academic Background (150–200 words)
Briefly describe your academic journey:
- Your current degree, university, and major
- Relevant coursework and grades
- Research experience (if any)
- Professional experience related to your field

> **Tip:** Be specific. Instead of "I studied biology," write "I completed a BSc in Molecular Biology at [University], specializing in microbial genetics with a GPA of 3.7/4.0."

### Section 2: Why This Field? (200–300 words)
Explain your academic motivation:
- What specific problem or question interests you?
- How did your academic or professional experience lead you to this field?
- What gap in your knowledge or skills do you want to fill?

> **Tip:** Show passion with specifics. Reference a particular research paper, a project you worked on, or a real-world problem you want to solve.

### Section 3: Why This University & Supervisor? (200–300 words)
This is the most critical section. Demonstrate that you have done your homework:
- Name the specific university and department
- Mention the professor or research group you want to work with
- Reference their published papers or ongoing projects
- Explain how their work aligns with your research interests

> **Critical:** Never write a generic study plan that could apply to any university. The committee can tell immediately.

### Section 4: Research Plan / Study Objectives (200–300 words)
Outline what you will accomplish during your studies:
- Specific research questions you want to explore
- Methodology you plan to use
- Expected outcomes or contributions
- For Master's/PhD: tentative thesis topic

### Section 5: Timeline (100–200 words)
Break your program into phases:

**Example for a 3-year Master's Program:**
| Semester | Focus |
|---|---|
| Semester 1 | Core coursework, Chinese language (HSK 4), literature review |
| Semester 2 | Advanced courses, research methodology, preliminary research |
| Semester 3 | Primary research, data collection, conference participation |
| Semester 4 | Data analysis, thesis writing |
| Semester 5–6 | Thesis defense, publication, graduation |

### Section 6: Post-Graduation Plans (100–150 words)
Explain what you will do after completing your degree:
- Return to your home country to contribute to its development
- Continue to PhD (if applying for Master's)
- Apply your knowledge in industry, government, or academia
- How the China experience will benefit your career

> **Pro Tip:** Emphasizing "contribution to my home country" resonates strongly with the CSC committee, as cultural exchange and knowledge transfer are core goals of the scholarship.

## Study Plan Template

Here is a template you can adapt:

---

**Study Plan for [Program Name] at [University Name]**

**1. Academic Background**

I am currently a graduate of [University] with a [Degree] in [Major]. During my studies, I focused on [specific area] and achieved a GPA of [X]. My academic journey has been shaped by [relevant experience, projects, or coursework]. I have also [professional experience, internships, publications, etc.].

**2. Motivation**

My interest in [field] was sparked by [specific experience or event]. Through my studies, I discovered that [specific problem/question] remains understudied, particularly in [region/context]. I believe that deeper research in this area could [potential impact]. This motivates me to pursue advanced studies at [University].

**3. Why [University Name]?**

[University Name] is internationally renowned for its research in [field]. I am particularly drawn to the work of Prof. [Name] in the [Department], whose recent publications on [topic] align closely with my research interests. Specifically, their paper "[Paper Title]" published in [Journal] (2024) demonstrated [finding], which I would like to extend to [your proposed direction].

**4. Research Plan**

During my studies, I plan to investigate [research question]. I will employ [methodology] to [objective]. My expected outcomes include [list outcomes]. This research could contribute to [broader impact].

**5. Timeline**

[Semester-by-semester breakdown as shown above]

**6. Post-Graduation Plans**

Upon completing my degree, I plan to return to [country] where I will [contribute how]. The knowledge and skills gained at [University] will enable me to [specific contribution]. I also hope to maintain academic collaborations with my supervisors and contribute to [field] through publications and research.

## Common Study Plan Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You |
|---|---|
| Too vague / generic | Shows you did not research the university |
| Too short (<500 words) | Suggests lack of seriousness |
| Too long (>2,000 words) | Committee will not read all of it |
| No specific professor mentioned | Missed the most important connection |
| Grammar and spelling errors | Creates poor first impression |
| Copy-pasted from the internet | Plagiarism detection tools will flag it |
| No post-graduation plan | Committee questions your commitment |
