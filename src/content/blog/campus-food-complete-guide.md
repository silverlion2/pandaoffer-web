## University Canteens: Your Lifeline

Chinese university canteens (食堂) are not your average college cafeteria. They are sprawling, multi-floor food halls with dozens of vendors serving regional cuisine from every corner of China. Here's how they work:

### Floor-by-Floor Breakdown

| Floor | Price Range | What to Expect |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **1st Floor** | ¥8–12/meal | Basic but filling. Rice + 2 dishes. Always the most crowded at peak hours (11:30am–12:30pm, 5:30–6:30pm) |
| **2nd Floor** | ¥15–25/meal | Regional specialties — Sichuan malatang (麻辣烫), Lanzhou hand-pulled noodles (兰州拉面), Xinjiang lamb skewers, Korean bibimbap |
| **3rd Floor / Special** | ¥20–40/meal | Premium options. Western food, Korean BBQ, Japanese curry, custom stir-fry |

### Muslim Canteen (清真食堂)

Most 985-tier universities have a dedicated **halal canteen** (清真食堂). These are certified halal facilities serving lamb, beef, and chicken dishes — no pork. The food is often considered some of the best on campus. If you follow halal dietary requirements, ask about this during your university orientation.

### Night Snack Street (夜宵街)

Open from 9pm to 1am near most dorm areas, the night snack street is where campus social life happens after hours. BBQ skewers (烤串) at ¥1–3 each, fried rice (炒饭) for ¥10, and bubble milk tea (奶茶) for ¥5–15 from Mixue (蜜雪冰城).

## Food Delivery: Eating Like a King from Your Dorm

Online food delivery is not a luxury in China — it's a daily utility. Two apps dominate:

### Meituan (美团) — Red App
- Filter by **月销** (monthly sales) to find the most popular restaurants
- Average delivered meal: ¥15–30
- Join campus **外卖红包群** (delivery coupon WeChat groups) to save ¥3–5 per order

### Ele.me (饿了么) — Blue App
- Owned by Alibaba. Often has better new-user coupons
- Integrated with Alipay for seamless payment

### Pro Tips from Students
- **Group ordering (拼单)**: Order with dormmates to hit free delivery minimums
- **Breakfast delivery**: Yes, you can get soy milk and fried dough (豆浆油条) delivered at 7am for ¥8
- **Late night**: Delivery runs until 2–3am in most cities. BBQ, fried chicken, and milk tea are available
- **New accounts get ¥20+ in coupons** — Some students create new accounts using family members' phones

> **Budget warning:** Food delivery is convenient but typically costs ¥5–15 more per meal than eating in the canteen. Twice-daily delivery = an extra ¥300–900/month compared to canteen eating.

## Must-Try Chinese Street Food

Don't graduate without trying these, ranked by student popularity:

1. **煎饼果子 (Jiānbing)** — Breakfast crepe with egg, crunchy cracker, and sauce. ¥7–10. The best street breakfast in China
2. **烤串 (BBQ skewers)** — Lamb, chicken hearts, tofu, anything on a stick. ¥1–3 each. Late night essential
3. **肉夹馍 (Ròu jiā mó)** — Xi'an's "Chinese hamburger." Braised pork in crispy flatbread. ¥8–15
4. **臭豆腐 (Stinky tofu)** — Smells terrible, tastes incredible. ¥8–15. The Changsha version is legendary
5. **糖葫芦 (Candied hawthorn)** — Sweet, sour, crunchy berries on a stick. ¥5–10. Perfect winter snack
6. **麻辣烫 (Malatang)** — "Spicy soup" where you pick your own ingredients by weight. ¥15–25 for a full meal
7. **小龙虾 (Crayfish)** — Summer obsession. Share 2kg with friends for ¥100–150. Garlic butter or mala flavored
8. **奶茶 (Milk tea)** — Heytea, Nayuki for premium (¥20–30), Mixue (蜜雪冰城) for budget (¥5–8)

## Cooking in the Dorm (The Underground Economy)

Most dorms technically ban cooking appliances, but every student vlogger on Bilibili owns these "contraband" items:

- **Electric hot pot (电煮锅)**: ¥50–100 on Taobao. Noodles, hotpot, boiled eggs — essential
- **Mini rice cooker (电饭煲)**: ¥80–200. Rice, congee, steamed eggs
- **Electric kettle**: For instant noodles and the all-important hot water (热水)

> **Warning**: Dorm circuits often can't handle multiple appliances. If you trip the breaker, the whole floor loses power — and everyone knows who did it.

Some newer dorms have communal kitchens with proper stoves. If yours does, use it. Pool ingredients with dormmates — one person cooks, four people eat. Cost per person: ¥10–15.

## Grocery Shopping

| Store Type | Best For | Price Level |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Campus convenience store** | Drinks, snacks, instant coffee | $$ (overpriced but convenient) |
| **Wumart / Yonghui / RT-Mart** | Weekly groceries | $ |
| **Hema (盒马鲜生)** | Premium + 30min delivery | $$$ |
| **Sam's Club / Costco** | Bulk buying (share membership ¥260/yr) | $$ |
| **Taobao** | Shelf-stable items, coffee, condiments | $ (free shipping) |

**Pro tip**: Buy seasonal fruit from campus fruit stands. Imported fruit costs 3–5x more than local options. In summer, watermelons are ridiculously cheap (¥1–2 per 斤/0.5kg).
