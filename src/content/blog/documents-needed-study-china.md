## The Ultimate Document Checklist

Preparing your application to study in China involves gathering many documents from different sources. Missing even one item can delay your application by weeks or get it rejected entirely.

This definitive checklist covers **everything you need** — organized by when you need it.

---

## Phase 1: University Application Documents

These are the documents you need to submit when applying to Chinese universities:

### Essential for All Applicants

| # | Document | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Valid Passport** | At least 6 months validity; photocopy of bio page |
| 2 | **Passport Photos** | 6–10 copies, 48×33mm, white background, taken within last 6 months |
| 3 | **Highest Degree Certificate** | Original or notarized copy; must be translated into English or Chinese |
| 4 | **Academic Transcripts** | From your current/most recent institution; notarized and translated |
| 5 | **Personal Statement / Study Plan** | 800–1,500 words explaining your goals. Read our [Study Plan guide](/blog/how-to-write-csc-study-plan) |
| 6 | **Language Proficiency** | IELTS 6.0+ / TOEFL 80+ for English programs; HSK 4+ for Chinese programs |
| 7 | **Non-Criminal Record Certificate** | From your local police, dated within 6 months |

### Additional for Graduate Programs (Master's/PhD)

| # | Document | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 8 | **Two Recommendation Letters** | From professors or academic supervisors who know your work |
| 9 | **Research Proposal** | For PhD applicants; 1,000–3,000 words with methodology |
| 10 | **Pre-Acceptance Letter** | From your target supervisor (not always required but strongly recommended) |
| 11 | **Publications List** | If you have any published papers or conference presentations |

### Additional for Medical Programs (MBBS)

| # | Document | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | **High School Diploma** | Notarized and translated |
| 13 | **Science Subject Scores** | Biology, Chemistry, Physics from high school |
| 14 | **Medical Fitness Certificate** | Confirming you can handle clinical studies |

## Phase 2: Scholarship Application Documents (CSC & Others)

If applying for the CSC Scholarship or other funded programs, you need additional documents:

| # | Document | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 15 | **CSC Application Form** | Completed online at campuschina.org; printed with signature |
| 16 | **Foreigner Physical Examination Form** | Completed by an authorized medical facility; includes blood tests, chest X-ray, ECG |
| 17 | **Study Plan in China** | More detailed than the university personal statement |
| 18 | **Financial Guarantee** | Bank statements or sponsor letter (for some scholarship types) |

> **Important:** The CSC application requires both the CSC online form AND many universities' separate application forms. You may need to submit documents to two different portals.

---

## Phase 3: Visa Application Documents

After receiving your admission offer, you will need these for your visa:

| # | Document | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 19 | **Admission Notice** (录取通知书) | Original from your university |
| 20 | **JW201 or JW202 Form** | JW201 for scholarship students; JW202 for self-funded |
| 21 | **COVA Application Form** | Completed online at cova.cs.mfa.gov.cn |
| 22 | **Physical Examination Form** | Same as scholarship requirement (if not already submitted) |
| 23 | **Proof of Accommodation** | Dorm assignment letter or hotel booking |

Read our complete [Student Visa Guide](/blog/china-student-visa-x1-x2-guide) for the full visa process.

## Phase 4: Documents to Bring to China

Pack these in your **carry-on bag** (never in checked luggage):

| # | Document | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 24 | **Passport with Visa** | Your most important document |
| 25 | **Admission Notice** (original) | Needed for university registration |
| 26 | **JW Form** (original) | Needed for residence permit |
| 27 | **Physical Exam Results** (original) | May need re-examination in China, but bring originals |
| 28 | **10+ Passport Photos** | You will use them constantly in China — registration, bank accounts, residence permit |
| 29 | **Academic Certificates** (originals) | University may ask to verify originals during registration |
| 30 | **Cash** | ¥2,000–3,000 for initial expenses before mobile payments are set up |
| 31 | **Proof of Insurance** | If required by your program |

## Document Preparation Tips

### Translation & Notarization
- Most documents must be **translated into English or Chinese** by a certified translator
- Some countries require documents to be **notarized** by a public notary and then **apostilled** or **authenticated** by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs
- **Start this process early** — notarization and authentication can take 2–4 weeks in some countries

### Digital Copies
- **Scan everything** and save to cloud storage (Google Drive, iCloud, etc.)
- Keep scans organized in folders: "Application," "Visa," "Originals"
- Many Chinese universities now accept digital submissions first, with originals checked at registration

### Photo Requirements
- Standard size: **48mm × 33mm** (slightly larger than US passport photos)
- **White background**, no glasses, no hat, front-facing
- Get 20+ copies — you will use them for university registration, bank accounts, residence permits, dorm registration, and more

## Timeline: When to Prepare Each Document

| Timeline | Documents to Prepare |
|---|---|
| **6–8 months before** | Passport renewal (if needed), language test prep |
| **4–6 months before** | Transcripts, degree certificates, translations, notarizations |
| **3–4 months before** | Recommendation letters, study plan, CSC application |
| **2–3 months before** | Physical examination, non-criminal record |
| **1–2 months before** | Visa application documents |
| **2 weeks before** | Final document check, photo copies, digital backups |
