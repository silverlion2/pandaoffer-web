## Ethiopia & China: A Strong Partnership

Ethiopia has become one of the **top African source countries** for students in China, with over **5,000 Ethiopian students** currently enrolled. The China-Ethiopia relationship — strengthened by infrastructure projects like the Addis Ababa–Djibouti Railway and the African Union headquarters — has created strong government-to-government scholarship pipelines.

---

## CSC Scholarship for Ethiopians

### How to Apply

1. **Ethiopian Ministry of Education route**: The government nominates students for CSC — check the Ministry's annual announcement (usually November/December)
2. **Chinese Embassy in Addis Ababa** (Type A): Apply directly through the embassy
3. **University direct** (Type B): Apply to the university with CSC application simultaneously

### What It Covers

- Full tuition, accommodation, monthly stipend (¥3,000 Master's / ¥3,500 PhD), medical insurance, and a one-time settlement allowance

### Tips for Ethiopian Applicants

- **Government-nominated candidates** have the highest acceptance rate — pursue this route if possible
- **Engineering and technology** programs have the most CSC slots for Ethiopian students
- **Contact professors early** — September/October is ideal
- **English-taught programs** are widely available — Amharic speakers don't need Chinese proficiency for admission

## Visa Process from Ethiopia

1. Receive admission letter + JW202 form
2. Medical exam at an approved hospital in Addis Ababa
3. Apply at the **Chinese Embassy in Addis Ababa** (Bole area)
4. Documents: passport (6+ months validity), admission letter, JW202, medical report, passport photos, study plan
5. Processing: 5–7 business days
6. Cost: Approximately ETB 3,000–5,000

### Money Transfer
- **CBE (Commercial Bank of Ethiopia) → ICBC/Bank of China** — bank-to-bank transfer
- **WorldRemit** — available for ETB → CNY
- **Western Union** — offices in all major Ethiopian and Chinese cities

## Ethiopian Student Community

### Finding Your Community

- **Ethiopian Student Associations** at major universities (especially in Beijing, Wuhan, Shanghai)
- **Ethiopian restaurants**: Available in Beijing (Sanlitun area) and Guangzhou
- **Orthodox churches**: Ethiopian Orthodox communities exist in Beijing and Shanghai
- **Coffee ceremony**: Bring your jebena and Ethiopian coffee — it's a great way to make friends and share culture
- **Telegram/WhatsApp groups**: Search "Ethiopian Students in China" — very active communities

### Cultural Tips

- **Coffee culture connects**: Both Ethiopia and China have deep coffee/tea cultures — use this as a bonding tool
- **Injera ingredients**: Teff is available via Taobao, and some African shops in Guangzhou stock it
- **Spices**: Berbere and mitmita are hard to find — bring a supply from home or order via Taobao
- **Weather**: Ethiopian highland students (Addis climate) adapt well to central/southern Chinese cities. Northern winters (Harbin, Beijing) will be significantly colder

### Best Cities for Ethiopian Students

1. **Beijing** — Largest Ethiopian community, embassy access
2. **Wuhan** — Very affordable, strong engineering programs
3. **Shanghai** — Career opportunities, international environment
4. **Chengdu** — Affordable, relaxed lifestyle, growing tech sector

## Career Path After Graduation

- **China-Ethiopia Belt & Road projects** need bilingual engineering graduates
- **Ethiopian Airlines** (Africa's largest airline) values China-experienced professionals
- **Diplomatic and trade roles**: Ethiopia hosts the AU — China expertise is highly valued
- **Tech sector**: Addis Ababa's tech ecosystem values Chinese-educated CS graduates
