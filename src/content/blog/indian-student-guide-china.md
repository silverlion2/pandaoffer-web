## India → China: The Education Pathway

Over **23,000 Indian students** study in China — the vast majority in **MBBS programs**. China offers WHO-recognized, NMC-approved medical degrees at a fraction of the cost of private medical colleges in India. Beyond medicine, engineering, business, and Chinese language programs are growing in popularity.

## MBBS in China for Indian Students

### NMC (National Medical Commission) Recognition

This is the **single most important factor** for Indian medical students. Only study at NMC-recognized universities. You must pass the **FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination)** or **NExT exam** after returning to practice in India.

### Top NMC-Recognized Universities

| University | City | Tuition/yr (USD) | Medium |
|-----------|------|-----------------|--------|
| **Fudan University** | Shanghai | $6,900 | English |

| **Wuhan University** | Wuhan | $5,200 | English |
| **Southeast University** | Nanjing | $4,500 | English |
| **China Medical University** | Shenyang | $4,800 | English |
| **Dalian Medical University** | Dalian | $4,500 | English |
| **Jiangsu University** | Zhenjiang | $3,800 | English |

### Important Rules for Indian MBBS Students

- **NEET qualification is mandatory** — even for studying abroad, Indian students must clear NEET
- **6-year MBBS program** (5 years + 1 year internship)
- **Clinical rotations in China** are in Chinese — students must learn functional medical Chinese
- **FMGE/NExT pass rates** vary by university — research this before choosing

> **Warning**: Some agents in India promote non-NMC-recognized universities with misleading claims. **Always verify on the NMC website** before paying any deposit.

## CSC Scholarship for Indians

### Application Routes

1. **Indian Embassy in Beijing** (Type A) — limited slots for Indian students
2. **Directly to the university** (Type B) — most common route for Indians
3. **Provincial scholarships** — Jiangsu, Zhejiang, and Shanghai offer provincial government scholarships

### Tips

- CSC is **more competitive for Indian students** than for African/Pakistani students — fewer government-nominated slots
- **Direct university application (Type B)** is your best bet
- **Provincial scholarships** often have less competition — check Jiangsu Government Scholarship, Zhejiang Provincial Scholarship
- For research programs, a **professor's pre-acceptance letter** is critical

## Visa Process from India

### Step by Step

1. Receive admission letter + JW202 form
2. Complete physical exam (at approved hospital — SRL/Metropolis labs accepted)
3. Apply at **Chinese Embassy in New Delhi** or **Consulates in Mumbai/Kolkata/Chennai**
4. Documents: passport, admission letter, JW202, NEET scorecard (for MBBS), physical exam, photos
5. Processing: 4–7 business days
6. Cost: Approximately ₹3,000–5,000

### Indian-Specific Notes

- **PCC (Police Clearance Certificate)** may be required — apply early from your local passport office
- **Apostille/attestation** of documents may be needed — check with your specific university

### Money Transfer
- **Wise (TransferWise)** — best rates for INR → CNY
- **Western Union** — reliable but fees are higher
- **Indian bank wire (SBI/HDFC → ICBC)** — possible but takes 3–5 business days
- **UPI doesn't work in China** — you'll use WeChat Pay and Alipay instead

## Indian Student Community

### Finding Your People

- **Indian Student Associations** at most major Chinese universities
- **Diwali and Holi celebrations** — organized by student associations, often with university support
- **Indian restaurants**: Available in Beijing (Connaught Place area), Shanghai (Jing'an/Hongqiao), and Guangzhou
- **Cricket**: South Asian student cricket is big — find your university's team
- **WhatsApp/Telegram groups**: "Indian Students in [City Name]" — very active

### Food Survival

- **Indian grocery stores**: Major cities have stores selling dal, atta, spices, and masalas
- **Taobao**: Search "Indian spices" (印度香料) — most basics are available online
- **Cooking is essential**: Most Indian students in China cook regularly — dorm kitchens become social hubs
- **Chinese food similarities**: Sichuan and Hunan cuisines are spicy — you'll find some familiar heat levels
- **Vegetarian challenge**: Chinese food is meat-heavy — vegetarian students need to learn "不要肉" (bú yào ròu = no meat) and "素食" (sùshí = vegetarian) immediately
