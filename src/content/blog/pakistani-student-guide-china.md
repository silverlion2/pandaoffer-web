## Pakistan → China: A Special Relationship

Pakistan and China share one of the strongest bilateral relationships in the world — and education is a massive part of it. Over **28,000 Pakistani students** currently study in China, making Pakistan the **#1 source country for international students in China**. CPEC (China-Pakistan Economic Corridor) has created enormous demand for Chinese-educated Pakistani professionals.

## CSC Scholarship for Pakistanis

### The HEC Route (Most Common)

Unlike most countries, Pakistani students primarily apply for CSC through **HEC (Higher Education Commission)** rather than the Chinese Embassy:

1. **HEC announces CSC slots** — usually November/December
2. **Apply on the HEC portal** — deadline typically January
3. **HEC shortlists candidates** → forwards to CSC
4. **CSC confirms** → university admission follows

### What CSC Covers

- Full tuition waiver
- Free accommodation (or stipend)
- Monthly stipend: ¥3,000 (Master's) / ¥3,500 (PhD)
- Medical insurance
- One-time settlement allowance

### Tips for Pakistani Applicants

- **HEC slots fill fast** — apply within the first week of the portal opening
- **Research experience** is critical for PhD applicants — publications help enormously
- **Professor contact**: Email 10–15 professors with your research proposal. A pre-acceptance letter makes you nearly guaranteed
- **IELTS is not always required** — many programs accept HEC-certified English proficiency
- **16-year education requirement**: Some Chinese universities require 16 years of education for Master's — if you have 14, consider a bridging program

## MBBS in China for Pakistanis

### PM&DC Recognition — Critical!

The **Pakistan Medical & Dental Council** maintains a list of recognized Chinese medical universities. **Only study at PM&DC-recognized institutions** — otherwise your degree won't be valid in Pakistan.

### Top PM&DC-Recognized Universities

| University | City | Tuition/yr | Notes |
|-----------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Dalian Medical University** | Dalian | $5,000 | Very popular with Pakistanis |
| **Jiangsu University** | Zhenjiang | $4,200 | Affordable, good teaching |
| **Southeast University** | Nanjing | $4,500 | 985 university, research-strong |
| **Wuhan University** | Wuhan | $5,800 | Beautiful campus, strong medical school |
| **China Medical University** | Shenyang | $5,000 | Purpose-built medical university |

> **Warning**: Always verify the latest PM&DC list before enrolling. Universities can lose recognition, and studying at an unrecognized institution means retaking exams in Pakistan.

## Visa Process from Pakistan

### Documents

1. Admission letter + JW202 form
2. Passport (6+ months validity)
3. Physical examination report (from approved hospital in Pakistan)
4. Passport photos (white background)
5. Study plan and CV

### Process

- Apply at **Chinese Embassy in Islamabad** or **Consulate in Karachi/Lahore**
- Processing: 5–7 business days
- Cost: PKR 3,000–5,000 approximately
- X1 visa (study >180 days) → convert to residence permit within 30 days of arrival

| Phone/Internet | ¥100 | PKR 4,000 |
| **Total** | **¥1,600–2,700** | **PKR 62,000–104,000** |

### Sending Money

- **JazzCash/Easypaisa → WeChat** — not direct, use intermediaries
- **Western Union** — reliable but fees are high
- **Wise (TransferWise)** — best rates for PKR → CNY
- **Hundi/Hawala** — common among Pakistani students but not recommended (legal risks)

## Pakistani Student Community

### What to Expect

- **Pakistani Student Associations** at nearly every Chinese university
- **Friday prayers (Jummah)**: Mosques available in all major cities — Beijing, Shanghai, Xi'an, and Guangzhou have historic mosques
- **Halal food**: 清真 (Qīngzhēn) restaurants are widespread — China's Hui Muslim community ensures halal options are available almost everywhere
- **Cricket**: Find other South Asian students — impromptu cricket matches happen regularly
- **WhatsApp/Facebook groups**: Search "Pakistani Students in [City]"

### Best Cities for Pakistani Students

1. **Xi'an** — Historic Silk Road connection, large Muslim community, great food overlap with Pakistani cuisine
2. **Beijing** — Largest Pakistani student community
3. **Wuhan** — Affordable, huge international student body
4. **Nanjing** — Good medical universities nearby

## Food & Cultural Tips

### Halal Food in China

- **清真 (Qīngzhēn)** = Halal — look for this symbol on restaurant signs
- **Lanzhou noodles** (兰州拉面) — halal, cheap (¥12–18), available everywhere
- **Xinjiang restaurants** — halal meat, naan bread, and pilaf rice — closest to Pakistani food
- **Cook at home**: Most dorms have kitchens. Pakistani spices are available in major cities or via Taobao

### Cultural Similarities

- **Hospitality culture**: Chinese and Pakistani cultures both value hosting and generosity
- **Tea culture**: China runs on tea, just like Pakistan!
- **Family values**: Family-oriented culture will feel familiar
- **Respect for elders**: Very similar to Pakistani cultural norms
