## Indonesia & China: Growing Education Ties

Indonesia is one of the fastest-growing source countries for international students in China, with over **14,000 Indonesian students** currently enrolled. The combination of geographic proximity, affordable education, CSC and LPDP scholarships, and China's rising global importance makes it an increasingly attractive destination.

---

## Scholarship Options

### CSC Scholarship

- **Apply through Indonesian Embassy in Beijing** (Type A) or directly to universities (Type B)
- Covers: tuition, accommodation, monthly stipend (¥3,000 Master's / ¥3,500 PhD), medical insurance
- Deadline: Usually January–March
- **Pre-acceptance letter from a professor** significantly increases chances

### LPDP Scholarship (Indonesian Government)

- Indonesian students can also apply for **LPDP (Lembaga Pengelola Dana Pendidikan)** to fund studies at top Chinese universities
- LPDP covers: tuition, living allowance, return flights, thesis support
- Chinese universities on LPDP's approved list include Tsinghua, PKU, ZJU, Fudan, and SJTU
- **Tip**: You can apply for both CSC and LPDP simultaneously — they cover different expenses

## Visa Process from Indonesia

1. Receive admission letter + JW202
2. Physical exam at approved hospital (Jakarta/Surabaya)
3. Apply at **Chinese Embassy in Jakarta** or **Consulates in Surabaya/Medan/Denpasar**
4. Documents: passport, admission letter, JW202, health exam, photos, study plan
5. Processing: 4–5 business days
6. Cost: Approximately IDR 500,000–800,000

## Cultural Connections

### Why Indonesia → China Feels Natural

- **Food**: Indonesian and Chinese cuisines share many ingredients — soy sauce, noodles, tofu, rice
- **Chinese-Indonesian community**: Indonesia has the world's largest overseas Chinese population — cultural familiarity runs deep
- **Muslim community**: Over 23 million Muslims in China — halal food (清真) is widely available
- **Climate**: Southern Chinese cities (Guangzhou, Kunming, Xiamen) have tropical weather similar to Indonesia
- **Indonesian restaurants**: Available in Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou

### Best Cities for Indonesian Students

1. **Xiamen** — Closest to Indonesia geographically, Fujian province has deep Indonesian connections
2. **Guangzhou** — Tropical climate, large international community
3. **Beijing** — Largest Indonesian student community, embassy access
4. **Shanghai** — Best for business and career opportunities
