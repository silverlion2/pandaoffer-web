## Two Visa Types: X1 and X2

China issues two types of student visas, and choosing the right one depends entirely on the length of your program:

| | X1 Visa | X2 Visa |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | Programs over 180 days | Programs under 180 days |
| **Typical use** | Bachelor's, Master's, PhD | Language courses, summer programs |
| **Residence permit** | Must apply within 30 days of arrival | Not required |
| **Validity** | Up to 5 years (with residence permit) | Duration of stay as printed on visa |
| **Re-entry** | Multiple entries with residence permit | Single or double entry |

> **Most degree-seeking students will need an X1 visa.** The X2 visa is primarily for short-term exchange or language students.

## Required Documents

### For Both X1 and X2 Visas

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| **Valid Passport** | At least 6 months validity remaining, with 1–2 blank pages |
| **Visa Application Form** | Completed online via the COVA system (cova.cs.mfa.gov.cn), printed and signed |
| **Passport Photo** | 48mm × 33mm, color, white background, no hat, front-facing |
| **Admission Notice** | Original + copy from your Chinese university |
| **JW201 or JW202 Form** | JW201 for scholarship students, JW202 for self-funded students |

### Additional for X1 Visa
| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| **Physical Examination Form** | Foreigner Physical Examination Form, completed and stamped by an authorized medical facility |
| **Proof of Financial Support** | Bank statements OR signed sponsorship letter (varies by embassy) |

### For Minors (Under 18)
- Birth certificate
- Copies of both parents' passports or IDs
- Parental consent form (notarized)

## Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Get Your Admission Notice & JW Form
Once accepted by a Chinese university, they will send you:
- An **Admission Notice** (录取通知书)
- A **JW201 or JW202 Form** (visa application form for studying in China)

These two documents are absolutely essential — you cannot start the visa process without them.

### Step 2: Complete the Physical Examination
Visit an authorized medical facility in your home country and complete the **Foreigner Physical Examination Form**. This includes:
- Blood tests (HIV, syphilis, hepatitis)
- Chest X-ray
- ECG
- General physical exam
- The form must be signed and stamped by the medical facility

> **Important:** Many countries require you to go to specific government-designated hospitals for this exam. Check with the Chinese embassy in your country.

### Step 3: Fill Out the Online Application (COVA)
1. Go to **cova.cs.mfa.gov.cn**
2. Create an account and select "Student Visa (X1 or X2)"
3. Fill in all personal information, travel details, and program information
4. Upload a digital photo
5. Print the completed form and sign it by hand

### Step 4: Submit to the Chinese Embassy/Consulate
Gather all your documents and submit them to:
- The **Chinese Embassy** in your home country, OR
- A **Chinese Visa Application Service Center (CVASC)** if your country has one

Some embassies accept walk-ins, while others require appointments. Check the specific requirements for your country.

### Step 5: Pay the Visa Fee & Wait

| Service Type | Processing Time | Approx. Fee |
|---|---|---|
| Regular | 4 working days | $140 USD (varies by country) |
| Express | 2–3 working days | +$20–30 |
| Rush | 1 working day | +$30–50 |

### Step 6: Collect Your Visa
Pick up your passport with the visa stamped in it. Double-check:
- Your name and passport number are correct
- The visa type (X1 or X2) matches your program
- The validity dates make sense for your travel plans

## After You Arrive: The Residence Permit (X1 Only)

If you have an X1 visa, you **must** apply for a **Temporary Residence Permit** within **30 days** of arriving in China. Here is how:

1. **Register at the local police station** within 24 hours of arrival (your university will usually help with this)
2. **Take a physical examination** at a Chinese hospital (even if you already did one in your home country)
3. **Gather documents:** Passport, admission notice, JW form, physical exam results, university enrollment certificate, passport photos
4. **Visit the local Exit-Entry Administration Bureau** (出入境管理局)
5. **Pay the fee** (~¥400–800) and wait 15 working days
6. **Collect your Residence Permit** — this will be a sticker in your passport

### What the Residence Permit Gives You
- Legal right to stay in China for the duration of your studies (up to 5 years)
- **Multiple entries** — travel freely in and out of China
- Ability to open a Chinese bank account (essential for daily life)
- Ability to sign apartment leases

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not applying early enough** — Apply at least 1 month before your departure, but not more than 3 months ahead
2. **Expired passport** — Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months from the application date
3. **Incomplete physical exam** — Missing a single test item can result in rejection
4. **Wrong JW form** — Make sure you have JW201 (scholarship) or JW202 (self-funded) as appropriate
5. **Overstaying your X2 visa** — If your program extends beyond 180 days, you need to convert to an X1 visa before your X2 expires
6. **Missing the 30-day window** — X1 holders who fail to apply for a residence permit within 30 days face fines and complications

## Visa Renewal & Extension

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| **Program extends beyond your residence permit** | Apply for renewal at the Exit-Entry Administration Bureau at least 30 days before expiry |
| **Changing universities** | You may need to re-apply for a new JW form and update your residence permit |
| **X2 expiring but program continues** | Must leave China and re-apply, or convert to X1 (complex — plan ahead) |
| **Lost passport with visa** | Report to police, get a new passport from your embassy, then re-apply for residence permit |

## Quick Checklist Before Your Flight

- [ ] Passport valid for 6+ months
- [ ] X1 or X2 visa stamped in passport
- [ ] Admission Notice (原件 — original)
- [ ] JW201/JW202 Form (original)
- [ ] Physical Examination Form (completed and stamped)
- [ ] 10 passport-sized photos (you will need them everywhere!)
- [ ] Copies of all documents (digital + physical)
- [ ] Cash: ¥2,000–3,000 for initial expenses
- [ ] Proof of accommodation (dorm assignment or hotel booking)
