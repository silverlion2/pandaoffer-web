## What is the CSC Scholarship?

The **Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)** is one of the most prestigious fully funded scholarship programs in the world, established by the Ministry of Education and managed by the **China Scholarship Council**. It is specifically designed to attract outstanding international students to study at Chinese universities, fostering international academic cooperation and cultural exchange.

Every year, thousands of students from over 180 countries receive CSC funding, making it one of the largest government scholarship programs globally. Whether you are pursuing a Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD degree, the CSC Scholarship can cover your **entire cost of study** in China.

---

## What Does the CSC Scholarship Cover?

The CSC Scholarship is genuinely **fully funded**. Here is exactly what it includes:

| Benefit | Details |
|---|---|
| **Tuition Fees** | 100% waived for the entire program duration |
| **Accommodation** | Free on-campus dormitory OR a monthly housing subsidy (up to ¥1,000/month for PhD) |
| **Monthly Stipend** | Undergrad: ¥2,500 · Master's: ¥3,000–3,500 · PhD: ¥3,500–5,000 |
| **Medical Insurance** | Comprehensive health insurance included |
| **Settlement Allowance** | One-time allowance for new arrivals at some universities |

> **Pro Tip:** Some universities and supervisors offer additional top-up funding on top of the CSC stipend, especially for PhD candidates in STEM fields. Always ask your prospective supervisor about this.

## Who is Eligible?

To be eligible for the CSC Scholarship in 2026, you must meet the following criteria:

- **Citizenship:** You must be a non-Chinese citizen with a valid passport
- **Health:** Be in good physical and mental health
- **No concurrent scholarship:** Not currently receiving any other Chinese government scholarship
- **Age requirements:**
  - Undergraduate: Under 25 years old with a high school diploma
  - Master's: Under 35 years old with a Bachelor's degree
  - PhD: Under 40 years old with a Master's degree
- **Language requirements:**
  - English-taught programs: TOEFL 80+ or IELTS 6.0+, or a certificate from your previous institution
  - Chinese-taught programs: HSK Level 4 or higher (some programs accept HSK 3 with a language prep year)

## Step-by-Step Application Process

The CSC application for 2026 typically opens in **December 2025** and closes by **April 2026**, with exact dates varying by university. Here is the complete process:

### Step 1: Research & Shortlist (November–January)
Choose your target universities and programs carefully. Check if they accept CSC scholars and whether they offer English-taught options. PandaOffer's AI Matcher can help you instantly evaluate your fit across 50+ universities.

### Step 2: Contact a Supervisor (For Master's/PhD)
This is the **most critical step** that most guides skip. For graduate programs, reaching out to a professor and securing a pre-acceptance letter can massively boost your chances. Write a concise email with your CV, research interests, and proposed study plan.

### Step 3: Online Application on CSC Portal
Create an account at **campuschina.org** or **studyinchina.csc.edu.cn**. Fill in your personal information, select the scholarship type (Type A for Embassy track, Type B for University track), and enter the correct agency number.

### Step 4: Apply to the University
Many universities require a **separate application** through their own admission systems. Some need a pre-admission letter from a supervisor before this step.

### Step 5: Prepare & Upload Documents
Gather and upload the following to both the CSC portal and the university's system:

1. ✅ Completed CSC Application Form (generated online)
2. ✅ Highest degree certificate + transcripts (notarized, translated to English or Chinese)
3. ✅ Foreigner Physical Examination Form (from an authorized hospital)
4. ✅ Valid passport copy
5. ✅ Study Plan or Research Proposal (800–1,500 words)
6. ✅ Two recommendation letters (from professors or academic supervisors)
7. ✅ Language proficiency certificates (HSK/IELTS/TOEFL)
8. ✅ Non-criminal record certificate (if required by the university)

### Step 6: Submit Before Deadline
For Type A (Embassy) scholarships, you may also need to submit hard copies to the Chinese embassy in your home country. Double-check country-specific requirements.

### Step 7: Wait for Results (June–July)
Results are typically announced between June and July 2026. Successful candidates receive an Admission Notice and JW201 form for visa processing.

## Common Mistakes That Kill Your Application

Based on years of helping students, here are the top mistakes we see:

1. **Generic Study Plan** — A copy-pasted or vague study plan is the #1 reason for rejection. Your plan must be specific to the university and program.
2. **Not contacting supervisors** — For graduate programs, a pre-acceptance letter is often the deciding factor.
3. **Weak recommendation letters** — Letters from family friends or unrelated people carry zero weight. Get letters from professors who know your academic work.
4. **Missing the deadline** — Deadlines are strict. Some universities close applications in February, even though the national deadline is April.
5. **Incorrect agency numbers** — Entering the wrong agency number on the CSC portal is a surprisingly common error that can invalidate your application.

## Types of CSC Scholarship Tracks

There are **two main tracks** for applying to the CSC Scholarship:

### Type A — Embassy Track
You apply through the Chinese Embassy in your home country. This track is ideal if your country has a bilateral education agreement with China. The embassy shortlists candidates and forwards applications to CSC.

### Type B — University Track
You apply directly to a Chinese university. The university evaluates your application and nominates you to CSC for final approval. This is the more common route and generally has a higher acceptance rate for well-prepared candidates.
