## Why Nigeria → China is Booming

Nigeria sends **more students to China than any other African country** — over 15,000 Nigerians currently study at Chinese universities. The combination of affordable tuition, CSC scholarships, and WHO-recognized MBBS programs makes China an increasingly popular alternative to the UK, US, and Canada.

## CSC Scholarship for Nigerians

### How It Works

The **Chinese Scholarship Council (CSC)** scholarship covers tuition, accommodation, monthly stipend (¥3,000 for Master's, ¥3,500 for PhD), and medical insurance.

### Nigeria-Specific Process

1. **Apply through the Nigerian Embassy** (Type A) — recommended, higher acceptance rate
2. **Apply directly to the university** (Type B) — also available
3. **CSC portal opens**: Usually December → closes January/February
4. **Required documents**: Passport, transcripts, study plan, recommendation letters, medical report

### Tips for Nigerian Applicants

- **Get a professor's pre-acceptance letter** — this is the single biggest factor for PhD applicants
- **Start contacting professors in September/October** — many Nigerian students wait too long
- **Your study plan matters more than your GPA** — write a specific, research-focused plan (see our [study plan guide](/blog/how-to-write-csc-study-plan))
- **WAEC/NECO results are accepted** — convert to GPA using standard scales

## Visa Process from Nigeria

### Step by Step

1. **Get your admission letter** + JW202 form from the university
2. **Complete the visa application** at the Chinese Embassy in Abuja or Consulate in Lagos
3. **Documents needed**: Passport (6+ months validity), admission letter, JW202, physical exam report (from approved hospitals), passport photos
4. **Processing time**: 5–10 business days
5. **Cost**: Approximately ₦30,000–50,000

### Physical Exam

Must be done at **approved hospitals** — Chinese Embassy in Abuja has the current list. The exam includes blood tests, chest X-ray, ECG, and general physical. Do this early — it can take 1–2 weeks for results.

## Money Matters

### Living Cost Comparison

| Expense | Monthly Cost (¥) | Monthly Cost (₦) |
|---------|------------------|------------------|
| Dorm (shared) | ¥800–1,500 | ₦180,000–340,000 |
| Food (canteen) | ¥800–1,200 | ₦180,000–270,000 |
| Transport | ¥100–300 | ₦23,000–68,000 |
| Phone/Internet | ¥100–200 | ₦23,000–45,000 |
| **Total** | **¥1,800–3,200** | **₦406,000–723,000** |

### Sending Money to China

- **Chipper Cash / LemFi** — most popular among Nigerian students
- **Western Union** — available but fees are higher
- **Bank transfer** — possible but slow (3–5 days)
- **Tip**: Open a Chinese bank account (ICBC or Bank of China) within your first week — you'll need it for WeChat Pay and Alipay

## Nigerian Student Community

### Finding Your People

- **Nigerian Students Union** exists at almost every major university in China
- **Nollywood movie nights** are common in student dorms
- **Jollof rice ingredients**: Most spices available at African shops in major cities (Beijing, Guangzhou, Shanghai)
- **Churches**: English-language services available in all major cities
- **WhatsApp groups**: Search "Nigerian Students [City Name] China" — there's a group for every city

### Guangzhou: Little Africa

Guangzhou has the largest African community in China. If you're studying nearby, weekend trips to Guangzhou give you access to African restaurants, markets, and a strong support network.

## Food & Cultural Adjustment

### What to Bring from Nigeria

- **Maggi/Knorr cubes** (available in China but expensive)
- **Ground crayfish** (hard to find)
- **Dried pepper** (Chinese pepper is different — milder)
- **Indomie** (available as "Indo Mie" in some stores, but Nigerian flavors are different)
- **Hair products** (limited selection in most Chinese cities)

### Adjusting to Chinese Food

- **Canteen food** is cheap (¥8–15 per meal) and safe to eat
- **Halal canteens** (清真食堂) are available at most universities with international students
- **Learn to cook**: Most dorms have shared kitchens. Nigerian students often cook together on weekends
- **Rice is everywhere** — you'll feel at home with that at least!

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|---------|
| Language barrier | Download [WeChat](/blog/wechat-vpn-digital-survival-guide), learn basic Chinese phrases, university provides free Chinese classes |
| Homesickness | Join Nigerian student union, video call family, find African restaurants |
| Cold weather (northern cities) | Buy thermals, layer up, consider southern cities like Guangzhou/Kunming |
| VPN for WhatsApp/Instagram | Get a reliable VPN before arriving (ExpressVPN or Astrill) |
| Racism/staring | Common in smaller cities, rarely malicious — most Chinese people are curious, not hostile |
