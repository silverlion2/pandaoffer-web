## Understanding WHO and NMC Recognition

Before choosing a university, it is critical to understand what "recognition" actually means:

### World Health Organization (WHO)
The WHO maintains the **World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS)** at wdoms.org. If a university is listed there, its graduates are eligible to sit for licensing exams (like the USMLE, PLAB, or AMC) in most countries worldwide.

### National Medical Commission (NMC) — India
The NMC (formerly MCI) does not officially "endorse" specific foreign universities. Instead, it requires Indian students to study at institutions listed on the **MOE's official list of 45 authorized universities** for English-medium MBBS. Students must also pass the FMGE/NExT exam upon returning to India.

> **⚠️ Critical Warning:** Always check the latest MOE list and WDOMS listing before paying any deposits. Some agents promote non-listed universities that will NOT qualify you for licensing exams.

## Program Structure: What to Expect

The MBBS program in China for international students typically follows this structure:

| Year | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Year 1** | Chinese language (HSK prep) + Basic sciences (Anatomy, Biochemistry) |
| **Year 2–3** | Pre-clinical subjects (Pathology, Pharmacology, Microbiology) |
| **Year 4–5** | Clinical rotations at teaching hospitals |
| **Year 6** | Compulsory clinical internship (this is required for NMC eligibility) |

### HSK Requirement (Important Update for 2026)
Starting from recent academic years, all international MBBS students must achieve **HSK Level 4** (Chinese language proficiency) to receive their final degree certificate, regardless of the English medium of instruction. This is because clinical rotations in Years 4–6 involve patient interaction in Mandarin.

> **Our Advice:** Start learning basic Mandarin before you arrive. Many universities offer a language preparatory year that counts toward your HSK requirement.

## How to Verify a University's Recognition

Follow these steps before committing to any university:

1. **Check the MOE list:** Visit the MOE website for the official list of 45 universities authorized to enroll international MBBS students
2. **Search WDOMS:** Go to wdoms.org and search for the university name. If it appears with a green checkmark, it is WHO-recognized
3. **Check your home country:** Visit your country's medical licensing body (NMC for India, GMC for UK, ECFMG for USA) and verify their specific requirements
4. **Ask for proof:** Request the university to provide their MOE authorization number

## Country-Specific Advice

### 🇮🇳 Indian Students
- Ensure your university is on the MOE's 45-university list — this is non-negotiable for NMC eligibility
- You must complete the full 6-year program including the 1-year internship in China
- Prepare for the FMGE/NExT exam after returning to India
- CSC Scholarship can cover your entire tuition — check your eligibility with our AI Matcher

### 🇵🇰 Pakistani Students
- PMC (Pakistan Medical Commission) recognizes degrees from WDOMS-listed universities
- Many universities have dedicated Pakistani student communities
- CSC Embassy track through the Pakistani embassy has good acceptance rates

### 🇳🇬 Nigerian / African Students
- MDCN (Medical and Dental Council of Nigeria) recognizes WDOMS-listed universities
- Several Chinese universities offer generous scholarships specifically for African students
- Look for universities with established African student associations for community support
