## Why Do a PhD in China?

China's research output has exploded — it now publishes more scientific papers than any other country and is #1 in high-impact research in fields like AI, materials science, and engineering. For PhD candidates, this means:

- **Fully funded programs**: CSC covers tuition, accommodation, and ¥3,500/month stipend
- **World-class labs**: Cutting-edge facilities, especially in STEM
- **Publication opportunities**: Chinese supervisors push for publications in high-impact journals
- **3–4 year programs**: Shorter than many Western PhD programs (US is 5–7 years)
- **Growing international recognition**: Chinese PhD degrees are increasingly valued globally

## CSC Doctoral Scholarship

### What It Covers

| Benefit | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| **Tuition** | Fully waived |
| **Accommodation** | Free university dorm or ¥1,000/month housing allowance |
| **Monthly stipend** | ¥3,500/month (~$480 USD) |
| **Medical insurance** | Included |
| **Settlement allowance** | One-time ¥1,500 |

### Eligibility

- Non-Chinese citizen in good health
- Under 35 years old (for doctoral programs)
- Master's degree (or about to complete one)
- Strong academic record
- English proficiency (IELTS 6.0+ or equivalent) for English programs
- Pre-acceptance from a Chinese supervisor is **strongly recommended**

## How to Find a Supervisor

This is the **most critical step** in your PhD application. A pre-acceptance letter from a professor can make or break your CSC application.

### Step-by-Step Supervisor Search

1. **Research the department**: Go to the university's English website → Faculty → your research area
2. **Read their recent papers**: Use Google Scholar or Semantic Scholar to find active researchers
3. **Write a professional email**:

```
Subject: PhD Inquiry — [Your Research Topic] — [Your Name], [Your Country]

Dear Professor [Name],

I am [Your Name] from [Country], currently completing my Master's in [Field] at [University]. I am writing to inquire about PhD opportunities in your research group for the Fall 2026 intake.

My research interests align with your work on [specific topic from their recent paper]. I have [brief relevant experience — publications, projects, thesis topic].

I have attached my CV and a brief research proposal for your reference. I would be grateful for the opportunity to discuss potential research directions under your supervision.

I am also applying for the CSC Scholarship and would greatly appreciate your support.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

4. **Follow up**: If no response in 2 weeks, send a polite follow-up
5. **Contact 5–10 professors**: Don't put all your eggs in one basket

### Red Flags to Watch For
- Professor hasn't published in 2+ years (may be inactive)
- No current PhD students listed on their page
- Asks for money before acceptance (scam)

## PhD Life in China: What to Expect

### Academic Expectations
- **Publications are king**: Your supervisor will push for 2–3 SCI papers minimum before graduation
- **Lab time**: STEM PhD students often work 60+ hours/week in labs
- **Regular seminars**: Weekly group meetings and department seminars
- **Teaching**: Some programs require TA duties

### Financial Reality
- ¥3,500/month CSC stipend covers living expenses in most cities
- Many supervisors provide **additional research allowances** (¥500–2,000/month)
- Conference travel is usually funded by the university or supervisor's grants
- Part-time work is technically restricted on a student visa

### Social Life
- PhD students often live in dedicated graduate dorms
- International PhD communities are small but tight-knit
- University gyms, libraries, and cafeterias are excellent
- PhD student associations organize social events and cultural exchanges
