## Scholarship Comparison

### 🇨🇳 China: CSC Scholarship
- **Coverage**: Tuition + accommodation + ¥3,000–3,500/month stipend + insurance
- **Availability**: 10,000+ scholarships for international students annually
- **Competitiveness**: Moderate — pre-acceptance letter from professor is key
- **Application**: Through Chinese embassies or directly to universities

### 🇰🇷 South Korea: KGSP / GKS
- **Coverage**: Tuition + housing + ₩900,000/month stipend + insurance + flight
- **Availability**: ~1,500 scholarships annually
- **Competitiveness**: Very competitive — under 10% acceptance rate
- **Requirement**: 1-year mandatory Korean language program before degree study

### 🇲🇾 Malaysia: MIS & MTCP
- **Coverage**: Partial to full tuition waiver
- **Availability**: Limited — typically for government partner countries
- **Competitiveness**: Moderate
- **Note**: Many Malaysian private universities offer institutional scholarships

**Winner: China** — Most scholarships available, highest monthly stipend, widest eligibility.

## The Bottom Line

**China** wins on value — the most scholarships, lowest costs, strongest research output, and access to the world's second-largest economy. It's the best choice for STEM, medicine, and research-focused students.

**South Korea** wins on lifestyle — K-culture, excellent internet, post-study work visa, and a thriving IT industry. Best for students interested in business, tech, and East Asian culture.

**Malaysia** wins on accessibility — English-medium programs, tropical climate, halal food everywhere, and a gateway to the ASEAN market. Best for students who want an affordable, comfortable Asian experience.
