## Who These China Study Tours Are For

Different groups need different route logic. A high school group needs safety, pacing, and future university orientation. An MBA or EMBA group needs market insight, operator conversations, and structured discussion. A healthcare delegation needs hospital access, international department flow, and service model comparison.

| Group Type | Best Route Focus |
|---|---|
| Middle school and high school groups | Campus exposure, Mandarin practice, culture, student life |
| University students | Major exploration, tech ecosystem, city research tasks |
| MBA and EMBA teams | Company visits, executive exchange, market entry cases |
| Healthcare managers | Hospital operations, patient service flow, premium care models |
| Investors and industry groups | AI, healthcare, medtech, robotics, digital economy |
| Families | Safe route planning, campus preview, cost and admissions briefing |

## Healthcare Industry Study Tour Route

The healthcare route is designed for investors, industry groups, hospital managers, medical education partners, and MBA or EMBA teams. It focuses on how China healthcare services actually operate, not just what the hospital looks like from the outside.

![China healthcare industry study tour route with hospital visit and market insight modules](/images/study-tours/healthcare-industry-study-tour.jpg)

Typical modules include:

- Doctor-led hospital department visit
- Expert sharing and Q&A
- Hospital operations and service flow
- Premium lobby or international department visit
- Private hospital, checkup center, or specialty chain comparison
- Biopharma, medical device, AI healthcare, genetic testing, or digital health company visit
- Healthcare market briefing and management roundtable

## AI Company and Tech Company Visit Routes

China's technology ecosystem is not one city or one company type. Shanghai is strong for enterprise tech and international business context. Hangzhou is ideal for platform economy and digital operations. Shenzhen is the best place to understand hardware speed, robotics, supply chain, and consumer electronics. Beijing is better for AI policy, research commercialization, and university-linked innovation.

Typical modules include:

- AI product demo and application case review
- Digital platform, e-commerce, fintech, or logistics briefing
- Robotics, IoT, smart device, or consumer electronics visit
- Smart factory, automation, supplier network, or industrial park visit
- Founder, operator, investor, or product manager Q&A
- Group capstone on China market positioning

## How to Choose the Right China Study Tour Route

Start with the learning outcome. A good study tour should answer one concrete question:

- "Should our students consider China for university?"
- "How does China's healthcare service model differ from ours?"
- "How do Chinese AI and tech companies commercialize products?"
- "What cities make sense for future degree study or business cooperation?"

Once the question is clear, the route becomes easier to design. The itinerary should include a mix of briefings, site visits, reflection tasks, and enough time for the group to process what they saw.

## Route Planning Checklist

Before requesting a route concept, prepare:

- Group size and age range
- Preferred dates and total duration
- Target cities
- Learning theme: campus, healthcare, AI, tech, culture, or mixed
- Group background: students, parents, executives, doctors, investors, teachers
- Language requirements
- Budget level
- Any required risk controls, dietary needs, accessibility needs, or guardian expectations
